Welcome

Welcome to Pickleball League! Pickleball is a fun and fast-paced sport that is growing in popularity across the world. It is a combination of tennis, badminton, and table tennis, and is played on a smaller court with a lighter ball and smaller paddles. Our pickleball league is open to players of all skill levels and is designed to provide a fun and competitive environment for players to enjoy the game. Whether you're a seasoned pro or just starting out, you're sure to have a great time playing pickleball with our league. So grab your paddle and join us for some fun on the court!
  1. Court: The Pickleball court is 20 x 44 feet for doubles and 20 x 34 feet for singles. The court is divided into two halves by a non-volley zone (also known as the kitchen) 7 feet from the net.
  2. Service: Service starts from the right-hand side of the court and alternates after each point. The server must serve the ball underhand and let it bounce once in the kitchen before hitting it.
  3. Scoring: Points are scored when the serving team makes a fault, the receiving team makes a fault, or the ball goes out of bounds. The first team to reach 11 points wins, but a team must win by two points.
  4. Let Serves: If a served ball touches the net but still lands in bounds, it is considered a let serve and is replayed.
  5. Non-Volley Zone: A player may not make a volley (hit the ball before it bounces) in the non-volley zone. This rule is known as the "no volley" or "kitchen" rule.
  6. Doubles: In doubles, the serving team must hit the ball to the diagonal court and rotate positions after each point.
  7. Substitutions: Players may substitute in and out of the game at any time.
  8. Equipment: Players use a paddle and a plastic ball that is similar to a wiffle ball.
These are the basic rules of Pickleball. There may be slight variations in different Pickleball communities, so it's important to check the rules of the specific organization or tournament you're playing in.
